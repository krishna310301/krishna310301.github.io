About

For two years at Tata Communications, I worked the side of infrastructure most people never see: 24/7 NOC rotations, night shifts, and high-severity incidents where a carrier circuit is down and everyone is waiting for a clear answer. BGP and MPLS failures, TCP/IP problems, DWDM optical faults, 100G escalations. I learned to stay calm, isolate the fault, and bring the service back inside the SLA.

That work changed how I think about systems. Once you’ve been the person paged at 2 AM, reliability stops being a feature you bolt on later and becomes the point. I don’t want to only react to failures; I want to build infrastructure so there are fewer of them, and so the next person on call isn’t left guessing.

I recently finished my MS in Computer Science at Indiana University Bloomington, and I build cloud infrastructure now with that same operations-first instinct. My projects are deliberately practical: an EKS reliability platform, a GitOps delivery platform, and a serverless uptime monitor. I care most about systems that are simple to operate, observable by default, cost-aware, and documented well enough to hand off without a meeting. It’s the kind of work I’d pick up on a weekend anyway.
